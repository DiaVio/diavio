The crash occurred on an east/west urban interstate highway in the westbound lanes. There were three westbound travel lanes bordered on both sides by paved shoulders with rumble strips. The westbound lanes were separated from eastbound traffic by a concrete traffic wall. The roadway was straight with a downgrade that varied from 3-4% for the duration of the crash/pre-crash area. Conditions were daylight and dry on a weekday morning with rush hour congestion approaching but not in a work zone. The speed limit was 89kph/55mph.
Vehicle #1, a 2002 Chevy Trailblazer was traveling in the left westbound interstate lane when it encountered Vehicle #2, a 2003 Chevy Trailblazer also in the left lane decelerating for traffic congestion. Vehicle #1 on recognition of the situation began to brake and swerved right then came left almost immediately, striking Vehicle #2 on its right side. The impact caused Vehicle #2 to roll Â¼ turn to its left. As it skidded forward on its left side the roof of the vehicle came into contact with the barrier wall. The vehicle rode up the wall and was righted on to its wheels before crossing all three lanes of traffic and coming to rest on the right shoulder. After initial impact Vehicle #1 veered to its right crossing the center and right travel lanes. It was then struck in the rear by the front of Vehicle #3, a 1988 Chevy S10 pickup truck. Both V1 and V3 came to rest on the right shoulder. All three vehicles were towed due to damage. Police Reported Travel Speed is Not reported for V1 and Not reported for V2.
